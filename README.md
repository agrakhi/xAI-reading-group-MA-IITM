# xAI-reading-group-MA-IITM
Reading group for Explainable AI at the Department of Mathematics, IIT Madras, led by [rakhi](https://github.com/agrakhi)

The course is adapted from [Su-In Lee at Washington State University.](https://sites.google.com/cs.washington.edu/xai/home?authuser=0)
## Overview

This repository supports a weekly reading group focused on **Explainable Artificial Intelligence (xAI)**, a subfield of machine learning that aims to make complex models more transparent and interpretable. Modern machine learning systems, such as deep neural networks and ensemble models, often achieve strong predictive performance but operate as “black boxes.” This lack of transparency can make it difficult for users and domain experts to trust or understand model decisions, particularly in high-stakes domains like healthcare and finance. This reading group explores both inherently interpretable models and post-hoc explanation techniques that help explain model behavior after training.

## Purpose

The goal of this reading group is to:

- Build intuition for explainability methods in machine learning  
- Understand current research in Explainable AI  
- Encourage collaborative learning and thoughtful discussion

## Schedule & Format

- **Semester:** January – May 2026  
- **Meetings:** Once per week  
- **Duration:** 60–75 minutes per session, Thursdays 3:30 - 5 pm  
- **Material:** One research paper (or chapter/article) per week  
- **Goal:** Shared understanding through discussion (not formal evaluation)

No prior research experience is required, but participants should be willing to think critically and engage deeply with the readings.

---

## Participant Roles (Rotating Weekly)
- **Paper Leader**  
  - Gives a 15-minute overview of the assigned paper  
- **Discussants**  
  - Each participant contributes:
    - One insight  
    - One discussion question  
- **Scribe**  
  - Posts a short written summary after each session
